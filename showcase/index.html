Animation system

Free GSAP. All cinematic.

No SplitText, no FLIP, no ScrollTrigger. Every effect is built from GSAP's free public API and CSS — and it shows.

✦
Word-split stagger
Headlines are split into <span class="wi"> elements at runtime. Each word staggers in at 0.06s intervals — the effect SplitText charges for, built manually and idempotently.
stagger: 0.06 · y: 28 → 0 · ease: expo.out
◧
Clip-path wipe
Eyebrow labels reveal left-to-right via clipPath. GPU-composited, zero reflow — the text simply emerges from the left edge with no layout shift.
inset(0 100% 0 0) → inset(0 0% 0 0) · 0.55s
◎
Blur-clear focus pull
Headline containers animate from filter: blur(7px) to clear simultaneously with the word stagger. A single compositing layer per headline — borrowed from Apple's product reveal cadence.
blur(7px) → blur(0px) · 0.7s · expo.out
⬡
3D depth — rotateX
Stats cards and two-column panels tilt in with per-element rotateX using GSAP's transformPerspective — sidestepping the preserve-3d propagation problem without touching the DOM hierarchy.
rotateX: 12 → 0 · transformPerspective: 900
◐
Spring back.out
Cards enter with a subtle overshoot that gives physical weight. Used on stat cards, gallery frames, and closing headlines — a small detail that makes the whole deck feel alive.
ease: back.out(1.5) · scale: 0.93 → 1
◉
Perlin gobo blobs
2D Perlin noise drives 3–4 radial gradient blobs every frame via gsap.ticker. Each blob has unique noise offsets so paths never repeat. Colors are keyed per theme — blue for Blue Hour, cyan for Deep Space, crimson for Noir.
pnoise(t + ox, oy) · 60fps ticker · GPU transform
☀
Sun + lens flares
Crissy Field and Golden Hour get a CSS solar disc with a pulsing box-shadow corona. Crissy Field adds 6 lens flare elements scattered along the optical axis using mix-blend-mode: screen for additive light.
box-shadow corona · screen blend · 6 flares on optical axis
◐
Film grain (Noir)
SVG feTurbulence with an animated seed attribute refreshes at 12fps. Full-viewport overlay at 7% opacity — classic photochemical grain texture without a video file.
feTurbulence · seed++ every 83ms · opacity: 0.07
◌
Floating dust (Obsidian)
55 particles — 28% terminal-green, 72% white — drift upward from the lower half of the screen. Each has a unique speed, drift, and start delay so the field never looks uniform.
55 particles · y: 0 → −(startPct+25)vh · ease: none

Design thinking

How the themes were made

Each theme started as a place, a time of day, or a material. Colors were sampled from photographs. The atmosphere followed the color.

Blue Hour

Indigo 20 minutes after dusk

The gradient was matched to a photograph of the Bay at 8:02pm in October — the exact moment the sky turns violet before going black. SF fog was the obvious companion: it's always there. The starfield uses 95 individually twinkling stars, each on a 1.8–6s GSAP cycle with random delay so no two blink together.

Crissy Field

The sun was a later addition

The original light theme had no sun — just the blue gradient. It was added after the gradient clearly suggested a cloudless noon sky. The 6 lens flares came from staring at Golden Gate photos at 11am: they're optically unavoidable. Each flare is positioned along the axis from sun (82vw, 12vh) through center and beyond.

Obsidian

The terminal has been black for 50 years

The question was: what if the dark theme had no color cast at all? Pure charcoal, near-black. The terminal green (#34C759 — exact macOS Terminal color) signals expertise to the right audience immediately. Dust particles replace stars because in a sealed dark room, what moves is dust.

Deep Space

The void should feel alive

200 stars instead of 95, and the nebula gobo colors are deliberately vivid — cyan, violet, nebula pink. Real Hubble imagery uses exactly these palettes. The fog is absent: the cosmic void is clean and silent, and the absence of atmosphere makes the starfield feel infinite rather than cozy.

Noir

Film grain was the missing piece

The crimson bloom came first — a single practical lamp in an otherwise dark room. Red gobo blobs in three values (red, crimson, dark red) avoided the flat-neon trap. The grain was added last: SVG feTurbulence at 12fps gives photochemical texture that no CSS filter can replicate. The fog returned from Blue Hour — in noir, it's mood, not weather.

Golden Hour

Sampled from a photograph at 6:18pm

The gradient stops came directly from a Napa vineyard photo in late September — amber at the horizon, warm white above. The sun is centered and low (not upper-corner like Crissy Field) because Golden Hour light is diffuse and everywhere, not directional. No lens flares for the same reason: you can't find the source.
